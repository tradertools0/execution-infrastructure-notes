# Replay System Notes

## Objective

The replay infrastructure is designed to reconstruct and evaluate historical execution behaviour under controlled simulation environments.

The objective is operational analysis, execution observation and infrastructure validation rather than prediction-focused replay activity.

---

## Core Replay Structure

Historical Execution Data
        ↓
Trade Reconstruction Layer
        ↓
Timestamp Synchronization
        ↓
Virtual Execution Environment
        ↓
Position State Tracking
        ↓
MAE / MFE Observation
        ↓
Execution Behaviour Analysis
        ↓
Operational Evaluation

---

## Historical Trade Reconstruction

The infrastructure reconstructs historical execution behaviour using structured execution records and timestamp sequencing.

Replay reconstruction may include:
- execution timestamps
- position lifecycle tracking
- entry and exit reconstruction
- exposure transitions
- execution sequencing

The objective is structured replay consistency during historical analysis.

---

## Timestamp Synchronization

Replay infrastructure continuously aligns historical execution events using internal time-sequencing logic.

Operational synchronization may include:
- execution ordering
- event sequencing
- multi-event coordination
- replay-state alignment

The objective is stable replay progression during historical reconstruction phases.

---

## Virtual Execution Environment

Replay execution operates inside a controlled virtual infrastructure environment.

The replay layer attempts to simulate:
- execution transitions
- exposure behaviour
- position-state movement
- operational sequencing
- execution pacing

The infrastructure prioritizes replay consistency rather than unrealistic execution precision.

---

## Position State Tracking

Replay infrastructure continuously tracks reconstructed position behaviour throughout the replay lifecycle.

Operational tracking may include:
- active exposure state
- trade-state transitions
- partial execution handling
- recovery-state behaviour
- trailing-state observation

The objective is continuous replay-state awareness during simulation activity.

---

## MAE / MFE Observation

Replay analysis continuously evaluates adverse and favourable execution movement after trade reconstruction.

Operational evaluation may include:
- maximum adverse excursion
- maximum favourable excursion
- exposure behaviour
- volatility interaction
- execution survivability observation

The objective is structured operational analysis during replay evaluation phases.

---

## Execution Behaviour Analysis

Replay infrastructure evaluates operational execution behaviour under historical market conditions.

Observed analysis areas may include:
- execution density
- volatility interaction
- directional instability
- recovery behaviour
- exposure escalation
- execution clustering

The objective is operational infrastructure analysis rather than isolated trade evaluation.

---

## Recovery Behaviour Observation

Replay environments may be used to evaluate recovery-state behaviour during unstable market conditions.

Observed behaviour may include:
- cascading recovery pressure
- correlated execution behaviour
- recovery pacing instability
- exposure propagation

Replay analysis helps identify unstable operational patterns before live execution deployment.

---

## Infrastructure Evaluation

Replay infrastructure may be used to evaluate:
- operational consistency
- exposure stability
- execution survivability
- protection-layer behaviour
- infrastructure adaptability

The objective is controlled infrastructure observation under historical execution conditions.

---

## Operational Philosophy

The replay infrastructure is not designed as a prediction engine.

The objective is structured operational analysis, execution-state observation and infrastructure validation under historical market conditions.

The infrastructure prioritizes:
- replay consistency
- operational awareness
- execution analysis
- exposure observation
- long-term infrastructure reliability
